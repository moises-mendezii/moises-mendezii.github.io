The Rise of Michelin Star Restaurants in New York City

By Moises Mendez II

Over the past 12 years, some of the best restaurants in New York City have been gaining the most sought after accolade, the Michelin Star.

New York City is home to some of the best restaurants in the world, one of them being Eleven Madison Park. The Michelin Guide has awarded Executive Chef Daniel Humm and Will Guidara three Michelin stars, its highest honor, and the fourth best restaurant in the world, according to Forbes. Only 72 (out of over 26,000) restaurants in New York City hold the honor of having at least one Michelin star and that number has slowly be on an upward climb and has almost doubled in the last 12 years.

Anastasia Soldano, a hospitality industry professional, has been working in the industry for over 20 years and gained experienced at restaurants all over the country and the coveted, Eleven Madison Park. “The service industry is seeing a trend of great, amazing restaurants being recognized,” she said. “Which is something that wasn’t so prevalent ten years ago.”

The Michelin Guide, which was started by two brothers in 1900, is the service industry’s most trusted list for top-notch dining not only in New York City, but around the world. So to be on this list means that your restaurant is doing something right.
